🧬 Discriminase

Design CRISPR guides that hit one target bacterium while sparing every commensal around it. Runs in your browser.

How to use

  1. Set your nuclease (Parameters). Cas12a or SpCas9, or a custom PAM — this decides which sites a guide can target.
  2. Build a panel of commensals to spare (Step 1). Add organisms on the right; your panel builds up on the left. Then press Build / load index.
  3. Choose your target (Step 2). Search a name and pick the exact strain, or paste/upload a sequence. Your choice shows on the left.
  4. Press Run (Step 3). You get guides that hit the target but avoid every commensal — download as CSV.

Ways to fill the panel

  • Load a panel → Examples — ready-made lists we ship (e.g. Gut microbiome, 52 species). Loads the organisms; you press Build. The genomes download in your browser the first time, then they're cached.
  • Load a panel → Prebuilt — a panel we already indexed for you: it's ready instantly, no Build step. (Appears here only when we've shipped one.)
  • Load a panel → Saved — panels you saved in this browser with “Save panel to browser”, so you can reuse them. Loads the list; you press Build.
  • Upload panel CSV — your own list, as a CSV with a taxid (or accession) column. Loads the list; you press Build.
  • One by one — search NCBI, or add a single accession / taxid, or upload FASTA genome files.
Saved vs Prebuilt, in one line: Saved = a list you kept and still need to Build. Prebuilt = already Built by us, instant.
💡 Off-target tolerance = how many mismatches still count as “would also cut a commensal.” Lower = stricter and safer. Nothing leaves your computer except genome downloads from NCBI.
⚙️ Parameters — Cas12a defaults (click to change nuclease / strictness)
