🧬 Discriminase

Find CRISPR guides that hit your target bacterium while sparing the surrounding microbiome — runs entirely in your browser.

How to use this — in 3 steps

  1. Set your nuclease (Parameters below). Pick Cas12a or SpCas9, or type a custom PAM. This decides which sites a guide can target.
  2. Choose commensals to spare — the bacteria you must not cut. Search NCBI and add them, upload FASTA files, or load a prebuilt panel, then press Build / load index.
  3. Pick your target — the bacterium you want to hit. Search by name and click the exact strain, or paste/upload a sequence.

Then press Run. You get guides that match the target but stay clear of every commensal — download them as CSV.

💡 Off-target tolerance = how many mismatches still count as “would also cut a commensal.” Lower = stricter and safer (fewer guides); higher = more permissive. Nothing you enter leaves your computer except genome downloads from NCBI.
